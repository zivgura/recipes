Privacy policy

This page describes how the Recipes web application (“the app”) handles information when you use Google sign-in.

What the app does

Recipe files live in Google Drive under the app publisher’s account. The publisher shares those files only with people they invite. The app is not a general-purpose Drive browser and is not meant to read your unrelated personal files.

Google account and OAuth

Google’s sign-in flow always issues credentials for the Google account that is signing in (that is how OAuth works). The app then uses Google’s Drive API read-only scope only so your browser can download the same recipe documents the publisher already shared with that account. You only see content you could already open in Drive because it was shared with you.

Google’s privacy policy applies to how Google processes account and API usage: Google Privacy Policy.

Data on your device

To work in your browser, the app may store:

  • A short-lived access token in session storage so you stay signed in until you close the browser tab or session, as implemented by the app.
  • Preferences (e.g. display settings or favorites) in local storage on your device only.

These values are not sent to a separate backend operated by this app for analytics or advertising.

No server-side account database

This project is deployed as a static site. The app does not create a separate user database on a custom server. Requests go between your browser and Google’s services; which recipe files are visible follows the publisher’s sharing settings in Drive.

Contact

For questions about this policy or the app, contact the person or organization that published this deployment.
